Executive Summary & Introduction

This comprehensive seven-module white paper presents the structural, legal, financial, ethical, and tax dimensions of CANVI Group's proprietary Shariah-compliant non-recourse property finance facility. Designed for UK residential real estate acquisition, this facility enables Muslim clients to achieve full home ownership without engaging in interest-based lending, personal guarantees, or conventional debt obligations.

Structured as a bespoke private financial arrangement, the facility is typically reserved for high-net-worth individuals (HNWIs) who meet strict eligibility criteria and seek a faith-compliant alternative to debt-based mortgages. It is not available to the general public or retail clients and is tailored to align with the complex personal, legal, and tax considerations of each client.

At its core, the model embodies a Wakalah-based investment structure, in which the client's capital is ethically managed to generate returns sufficient to fund the acquisition of a home. These returns extinguish the facility progressively over timeâ€”without imposing financial liability on the client in cases of market underperformance. Legal title is registered directly in the client's name from the outset, with no liens, leases, or encumbrances placed on the property.

This facility is distinct from Islamic mortgages (e.g., Murabaha, Ijara, or Diminishing Musharakah) in both form and function. It offers a unique, ethically rigorous alternative that eliminates riba (interest), gharar (uncertainty), and maysir (speculation), while delivering a transparent and contractually sound pathway to home ownership aligned with UK law and Islamic jurisprudence.

Strategic Context

Muslim professionals and families in the UK and globally face persistent barriers to acquiring property through traditional financial systems that conflict with Islamic beliefs. While Islamic mortgage products exist, many still replicate debt paradigms and embed cost structures that contravene both the letter and spirit of Shariah. The CANVI model was engineered as a response to this challengeâ€”delivering a non-debt, ethically screened, investment-led solution supported by legal robustness and commercial clarity.

Key Structural Features

Non-Recourse Facility

No obligation for personal repayment beyond initial capital. No risk of foreclosure, repossession, or credit enforcement.

Wakalah Investment Framework

Client capital is managed by CANVI as an agent (Wakil), deployed exclusively into halal, asset-backed, income-generating opportunities.

Full Legal Ownership from Day One

The residential property is registered in the client's name immediately, with no phased ownership or transfer schedule.

No Monthly Repayments

The facility is extinguished via capital returns over time, not through income-based instalments.

Shariah Integrity

Comprehensively avoids riba, gharar, and maysir, and adheres to the Maqasid al-Shariah in its design and execution.

UK Legal Validity

Structured under English law and implemented with full solicitor oversight, avoiding classification as a regulated mortgage or consumer credit product.
